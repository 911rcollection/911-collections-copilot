Part 1 · The Business

Why this system exists

Restoration work gets done fast. Getting paid for it is the slow part.

  • Money flows through insurance. Payment travels through adjusters and homeowners — not straight to you — so it's slow and easy to lose track of.
  • Payments get diverted. Carriers sometimes send the check to the homeowner instead of 911, and it never arrives.
  • Estimates get disputed. Adjusters approve less than you billed, and the gap sits unresolved for weeks.
  • Lien deadlines expire. Oregon gives 75 days, Washington 90 — miss it and your right to collect is gone forever.
  • It all lived in spreadsheets, email threads, and sticky notes. Nothing chased itself.
The cost of doing nothing: invoices quietly age past 90 days, checks vanish, and cash that's already been earned never comes home.

DSO in plain English: the average number of days between finishing a job and getting paid. Every day you shave off DSO is cash arriving sooner. The Copilot's whole purpose is to push this number down.
Why aging buckets matter: a fresh invoice (0–30 days) almost always gets paid. Once it crosses 90+ days, the odds drop fast. The system's job — and yours — is to keep invoices from ever reaching that far-right bucket.
Owner win Every dollar collected 30 days sooner is a dollar you can put back into crews, equipment, and payroll — instead of waiting on an insurer.
Part 2 · The Solution

How a job becomes cash — end to end

Here's the full journey. Most of it happens automatically; you step in only at the highlighted human moments.

1
Job is invoiced → "Zero Hour." The moment a restoration job is billed in Proven Jobs, the clock starts. This is the trigger for everything.
2
Job flows into the database. Claim, carrier, adjuster, homeowner and invoice details sync in automatically. (Content & Storage jobs are excluded — they never enter collections.)
3
Automated follow-up begins. A timed sequence of emails and texts goes out to the adjuster — Day 3, 7, 14, 21… — each screened by safety checks first.
4
AI reads every reply. When the adjuster responds, AI classifies it: payment confirmed, dispute, "I'll respond by Friday," out-of-office — and pauses or reroutes the cadence.
5
Homeowner stays informed. Gentle weekly progress updates keep them in the loop and cut down "where's my check?" calls.
6
Payment lands — or gets diverted. When money hits QuickBooks the invoice is marked paid. If a check went to the wrong party, an 8-step recovery kicks off.
7
👤 You step in. Anything the robot can't close — a stalled claim, a dispute, an aged diversion, a lien decision — lands in your Strike Queue as a phone call to make.
8
Everything is logged. Your call outcome is written back automatically, so the record is always complete and the next reminder knows what happened.

Part 2 · The Solution

Seven safety checks before every message

This is what keeps the automation from ever sending something awkward. Every message passes all seven gates first.

🎉 1 · Holiday Gate

Never sends on a US federal holiday.

✅ 2 · Paid Guard

Skips any job that's already paid or written off.

✋ 3 · Payment Stop

A manual "hold everything" switch you can flip on a case.

😴 4 · Snooze Guard

Pauses if the adjuster promised to respond by a date.

🏝️ 5 · Out-of-Office Gate

Waits if the adjuster is away, then resumes on their return.

⚖️ 6 · Disputed Gate

Stops routine chasing once a job is in dispute.

🚫 7 · Excluded Gate

Content & Storage jobs never enter collections at all.

Why you care These guards mean you can trust the system to run unattended. It won't text a customer on Thanksgiving or chase a bill that's already been paid.
Part 2 · The Solution

The AI that reads your mail

When an adjuster replies, AI (Google Gemini) reads it and sorts it into one of seven meanings — then the system reacts automatically.

💰 Payment confirmed

→ Updates the payment record and closes out the chase.

⚖️ Dispute

→ Flags the job as disputed and opens the resolution track.

🤝 Partial agreement

→ Records the approved amount and the remaining gap.

📅 "I'll respond by…"

→ Sets a snooze date and pauses follow-ups until then.

❓ Info request

→ Logs it and adds a task to your queue to reply.

🏝️ Out of office

→ Reads the return date and waits for the adjuster to be back.

Anything unclear is marked "Other" and sent to you for a human look — the AI never guesses when it isn't sure.
Part 2 · The Solution

What this looks like in real life

Six everyday situations and how the Copilot handles each one.

Routine follow-up

Invoice hits Zero Hour. Day 3 email, Day 7 email, Day 8 text… the adjuster is nudged automatically until they pay — no one lifts a finger.

"I'll get back to you Friday"

The adjuster replies with a date. AI reads it, pauses the cadence, and quietly resumes if Friday passes with no payment.

Payment diverted

A carrier mails the check to the homeowner instead of 911. The system detects it and runs an 8-step recovery from Day 0 to Day 30.

Estimate dispute

Adjuster approves less than billed. The gap is tracked and a step-by-step resolution timeline opens — supplement, re-inspection, appraisal.

Keeping the homeowner calm

Weekly progress emails go out on their own, so the homeowner feels informed and stops calling the office for updates.

Lien deadline looming

A job nears its 75/90-day window. It surfaces in the Lien Zone so an owner decides Regular / Silent / None before it's too late.

Part 4 · Your Daily Job

Your 10-minute morning routine

Do these four things every morning and the system stays healthy. That's the whole job in a nutshell.

1
Log in & scan the Dashboard. Look for anything that jumped — invoices sliding into 90+ days, new diversion alerts, a red number that wasn't there yesterday.
2
Check "My Tasks" for today. This is your personal list, pulled from six places at once — the queue, lien deadlines, disputes, diversions, AI inbox, and info requests.
3
Skim the AI Inbox. See what adjusters said overnight. Most are already handled by AI — you only act on the ones asking for something.
4
Work the Strike Queue — red first. Start at the top with P1 (Critical), make your calls, log the outcomes, and clear them. When the reds are gone, you're done.
That's it. If nothing is red and the queue is short, the automation has handled everything — enjoy the quiet. The system only pulls you in when a human voice is genuinely needed.
Part 4 · Your Daily Job

Working a task — the three buttons

Every card in the Strike Queue gives you the same three choices. Here's when to use each.

📞

Start Call

Opens a contact card with click-to-dial, the claim details, suggested talking points, a notes box, and an outcome dropdown. When you save, the call is logged automatically.

✅

Resolve

Done with this one? Pick a resolution type, add a quick note, and it drops off your queue — and a record is written to the history for you.

🗂️

View Case

Need the full story before you act? This opens the complete Case File — every message, payment, and note in one place.

The golden rule: you never have to write a log entry by hand. Whenever you Start a Call or Resolve a task, the system records it in the history and updates the case. Your job is the phone call and the outcome — not the paperwork.
Good to know There's no "Save" button on most screens — edits save the instant you make them. Made a mistake? Ctrl + Z undoes it.
Part 4 · Your Daily Job

Handling the tricky ones

Three situations you'll meet often — and the exact clicks for each.

Adjuster approves partial

1 · Open the case from the AI Inbox
2 · Actions → Log Carrier Response
3 · Choose Partial Agreement, enter the approved amount
4 · Pick Accept, Dispute, or Decide Later

Payment was diverted

1 · Click the diversion card on the Dashboard
2 · Watch the 8-step recovery run itself
3 · If it hits Day 31 unresolved, it becomes a P1 call in your queue
4 · Make the call, log the outcome

Lien deadline (owners)

1 · Open the Lien Zone
2 · Work the red rows first
3 · Choose Regular, Silent, or None
4 · Fill "Decision Made By" + notes — done before the window closes

When in doubt, escalate to Moshe. Lien filing choices and large disputes are business decisions. The system surfaces them early on purpose so there's always time to ask.
