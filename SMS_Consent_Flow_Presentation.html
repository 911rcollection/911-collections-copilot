How a customer gives SMS consent

Two ways to opt a customer in — a texted request they answer with YES, or a consent form (in person or by personalized link) — both write consent to the same Homeowner record, then gate every future text. Press Play or step through.

Prerequisite · who is eligible to be asked

The homeowner qualifies for an opt-in ask when…

  • ✓ Linked to a Job with Collections Status = Active
  • ✓ Has a mobile number on file (the SMS opt-in path texts it)
  • ✓ SMS Consent ≠ true — hasn't already opted in
  • ✓ Do Not Contact ≠ true and SMS Opt Out ≠ true
  • ✓ Not asked in the last 14 days (re-ask throttle)
S-16 checks all of these automatically. A PM asking in person only needs a not-yet-consented customer.
Option A · In person at onboarding
Path A

PM presents the form on a device

  • PM copies “SMS Consent Form Link” from the customer's Homeowner row
  • Opens it on a tablet / phone during work-authorization signing
  • Customer reads the consent statement (box unchecked by default)
  • Checks the box, enters mobile, submits
Consent is a separate, optional step from the service agreement — keeps it TCPA-clean.
Option B · Remote link
Path B

S-16 texts the opt-in — customer replies YES

  • S-16 runs daily, finds eligible homeowners (prerequisites above)
  • Sends an SMS: “911 Restoration: reply YES to get claim updates by text. Reply STOP to opt out.” and stamps Consent Request Sent = now
  • Customer replies YES → S-15 matches their mobile and records consent
  • Prefer a documented form instead? Send the branded link sms-optin.html?id=recXXXX (same capture as Option A)
US delivery requires the Twilio number's A2P 10DLC registration + US region enabled.
